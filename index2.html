Windfarm locator map

I was assigned what appeared as a simple locator map. However, I worked closely with the reported in gathering all the acreage map data for land leased to a company creating a windfarm across two Missouri counties. The acreage maps weren’t online, so I had to use physical images of the acreage maps, taken by the reporter from Howard County’s acreage map book.


I had to trace over each individual piece of land and ensure that each trace was proportional, as the images I had were taken at different distances and angles. I’m especially proud of this graphic, as it hides all that complexity with a simple and clear design.

Data analysis on vaccines in rural Missouri counties

I worked with the Columbia Missourian’s state government section in providing data analysis for a story they pitched on the state of vaccinations in rural counties. The data analysis helped guide them towards which counties they wanted to visit and report on. I also used the data in visualizations that I assigned to members of the graphics section.


There was a bunch of behind-the-scenes visualizations that I used to find any trends and potential leads for the story. The end result was that there weren’t any major trends, teaching me a valuable lesson: There’s often not a clear-cut trend in data.
